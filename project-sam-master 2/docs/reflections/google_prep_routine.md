# Google Prep Routine: "DSA First, Android Second"

**Purpose:**  
4-week intensive Google interview preparation with DSA as primary focus.  
Android development continues but with reduced time allocation.  
Balanced approach to prevent burnout while maximizing interview readiness.

---

## **Daily Structure**

### **7:00 – 9:00 AM — Block 1: Body + Mind Foundation**
- Wake, hydrate, cold rinse, eat protein
- Light 10 min walk or stair climb
- No screen till 9 AM  
**Objective:** Activate body to stabilize cognitive output

---

### **9:00 – 12:00 PM — Block 2: DSA Deep Dive (3 hours)**
- **9:00-10:30 AM**: Theory + Concept Review
  - Read/study 1 DSA concept (arrays, trees, graphs, DP, etc.)
  - Watch 1-2 LeetCode solution videos
  - Take notes in structured format
- **10:30-12:00 PM**: Practice Problems
  - Solve 2-3 LeetCode problems (mix of easy/medium)
  - Focus on implementation, not just understanding
  - Track progress in spreadsheet
**Objective:** Build DSA muscle memory and problem-solving speed

---

### **12:00 – 1:00 PM — Block 3: Lunch + Reset**
- Eat. No screen overload.
- Sit silently or loop one safe music track  
**Objective:** Mental reset before afternoon session

---

### **1:00 – 3:00 PM — Block 4: Android Development (2 hours)**
- **1:00-2:00 PM**: Core Android Work
  - Continue current Android project development
  - Focus on one feature/component per session
- **2:00-3:00 PM**: Android Learning
  - Study one Android concept (Jetpack Compose, architecture, etc.)
  - Practice implementation
**Objective:** Maintain Android skills while prioritizing DSA

---

### **3:00 – 4:00 PM — Block 4: Dead Work + Admin**
- Clean room, manage files, errands
- Update job applications, check emails
- Grit tasks, no mental stimulation  
**Objective:** Reclaim control + remove guilt load

---

### **4:00 – 6:00 PM — Block 5: DSA Practice Round 2 (2 hours)**
- **4:00-5:00 PM**: Mock Interview Practice
  - Solve 1-2 medium/hard problems under time pressure
  - Practice explaining solutions out loud
  - Record yourself if comfortable
- **5:00-6:00 PM**: Review + Analysis
  - Review today's problems and solutions
  - Identify patterns and common mistakes
  - Plan tomorrow's focus areas
**Objective:** Build interview confidence and problem-solving stamina

---

### **6:00 – 7:00 PM — Block 6: Backup Job Applications**
- Apply to 2-3 backup jobs (non-Google)
- Update resume if needed
- Send follow-up emails
**Objective:** Maintain backup options so Google isn't all-or-nothing

---

### **7:00 – 9:00 PM — Block 7: Depletion Drift**
- Do anything. Phone, music, video, etc.
- No introspection or analysis  
**Objective:** Let system vent without destabilizing

---

### **10:00 PM – Sleep — Shutdown Ritual**
- Shower, soft log, sit for 10 min, then bed
- No active thought or reflection in bed  
**Objective:** Light sedation + clean closure

---

## **Weekly DSA Focus Schedule**

### **Week 1: Arrays & Strings**
- Monday: Array manipulation, two pointers
- Tuesday: Sliding window, prefix sums
- Wednesday: String manipulation, palindromes
- Thursday: Sorting, searching
- Friday: Review + practice problems
- Weekend: Mock interview (2 problems, 1 hour)

### **Week 2: Linked Lists & Trees**
- Monday: Linked list operations
- Tuesday: Tree traversal (DFS/BFS)
- Wednesday: Binary search trees
- Thursday: Tree construction, validation
- Friday: Review + practice problems
- Weekend: Mock interview (2 problems, 1 hour)

### **Week 3: Graphs & Dynamic Programming**
- Monday: Graph representation, BFS/DFS
- Tuesday: Shortest path algorithms
- Wednesday: DP basics, memoization
- Thursday: Classic DP problems
- Friday: Review + practice problems
- Weekend: Mock interview (2 problems, 1 hour)

### **Week 4: Advanced Topics & Mock Interviews**
- Monday: Heaps, priority queues
- Tuesday: Advanced DP, backtracking
- Wednesday: System design basics
- Thursday: Mock interview (full 45 min)
- Friday: Review weak areas
- Weekend: Final mock interview (Google-style)

---

## **Non-Negotiable Rules**
- **DSA blocks are sacred - no skipping**
- **Android gets 2 hours max daily**
- **Track DSA progress daily (problems solved, concepts mastered)**
- **If 2 DSA days are missed → pause Android, focus only on DSA**
- **Backup job applications continue weekly**

---

## **Success Metrics**
- **Daily**: 3-4 DSA problems solved
- **Weekly**: 1 mock interview completed
- **Monthly**: Ready for Google first round
- **Android**: Maintain current skill level, no regression

---

**Note:** This routine prioritizes DSA while keeping Android skills fresh. The goal is to be interview-ready in 4 weeks while maintaining a balanced approach to prevent burnout.
